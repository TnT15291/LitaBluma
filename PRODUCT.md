# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Two distinct users sharing one app, on different cadences:

- **Parent (primary operator & decider):** a busy parent of a 4–10-year-old. Opens the app in short bursts — at bedtime, after dinner, on the commute — to record how the day went. They are also the payer and the keep-or-cancel decider. Their context is operational and time-pressured: "log today, see if it's working, move on."
- **Child (beneficiary):** 4–10 years old, often on a parent's phone or a shared tablet, sometimes pre-literate. They open the app to *see their garden grow* and feel recognized. Their context is emotional and playful, never administrative.

The job to be done: help a parent build a child's positive habits through recognition — and let the child feel that growth — without punishment, comparison, or shame.

## Product Purpose

LitaBluma turns everyday parenting recognition into a visible, growing garden. The parent records behavior (completed / tried / not-yet); positive moments feed a seed-point ledger and grow the child's garden; the parent defines rewards the child can unlock. Success is the daily loop becoming a habit for the parent (returning ≥3×/week) and the child internalizing positive behavior even without a reward. The core loop must deliver value with **no AI**; AI (encouragement, weekly letter) is the reason to *pay*, not the reason to *stay*.

## Brand Personality

Warm, nurturing, trustworthy. Three words: **gentle, encouraging, grounded.** The voice is a calm parent's voice — specific praise over generic "good job," never clinical, never gamified-hype. The product radiates *care*: for the child's feelings and for the parent's trust with their child's data.

## Anti-references

- **Gamified dopamine grinders** (Candy Crush, streak-shaming habit apps): no loss-aversion, no flashing rewards, no "don't break your streak" pressure. The garden is not a slot machine.
- **Parental-control / surveillance dashboards** (screen-time cops): parent mode is a calm workspace, not a monitoring console with red alerts and behavior charts that feel like a rap sheet.
- **Clinical / medical UI:** no diagnosis tone, no charts that pathologize a child.
- **SaaS-cream slop:** no warm-near-white parchment body, no hero-metric blocks, no identical icon-card grids, no per-section uppercase eyebrows.
- **Error-red for behavior:** red is reserved for genuine system faults. `not_yet` is neutral and invisible to the child.

## Design Principles

1. **Recognition over reward.** Celebrate effort and growth, never a score. The garden grows from positive moments; the child never sees a running point total as the main visual.
2. **Two worlds, one home.** The child's immersive garden and the parent's calm workspace are deliberately different aesthetics. Crossing into parent mode is a felt boundary (the parental gate), not a tab switch.
3. **The child never sees failure.** `not_yet` is invisible to the child; no red, no deficit language, no negative feed. Only positive moments surface in child mode.
4. **Privacy is visible care.** Minimal child data, system avatars only, consent and delete are first-class flows — privacy is felt as part of the brand, not buried in a policy page.
5. **The app suggests; the parent decides.** Calm operational clarity without surveillance. The app proposes (taper points, update on band change) but never auto-changes a child's checklist, points, or rewards.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG 2.1 AA** contrast for all text and meaningful UI; body ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1. Behavior outcomes never rely on color alone (icon + label + color).
- **Reduced motion** is a first-class alternative on every animation (crossfade/instant), not an afterthought.
- **Large touch targets in child mode:** primary tappables ≥56px; comfortable for small, imprecise hands.
- **Readable, friendly type:** generous size and line-height; a warm, rounded face for child mode that stays legible for new readers. Mobile-first; child mode also usable on a shared tablet.
